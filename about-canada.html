About Canada

Canada, the world's second largest country, has plenty to be proud of: gorgeous natural parks, a rich and diverse culture and heritage, a three-seas coastline, Old World charm and New World values, and hockey. Canada welcomes visitors with open arms and vast open spaces. Do you intend to take a vacation or a getaway? Do you wish to learn more about your pleasant northern neighbour? You want to make an impression on a stunning Canadian you just met? This section offers general, essential, must-know information for anybody interested in Canada.

MAJOR CITIES AND TOURIST LANDMARKS

Cities in Canada

Toronto is the biggest city in Canada, with a population of 5,429,524 people. It is the capital of Ontario and boasts the world's second biggest number of international residents. Toronto is the most cosmopolitan city in the world since no single nationality has a considerable majority. The CN Tower, the Toronto Islands, and Ripley's Aquarium of Canada are all popular attractions.

Canada Landmarks for Tourists

The world's most iconic waterfall and Canada's most visited tourist destination, Niagara Falls. It is about an hour's drive from Toronto and is widely regarded as one of the world's natural marvels.
Banff National Park - This park is home to many untouched natural treasures, including the famed Lake Louise. It is often regarded as one of the most beautiful mountainous locations in the world.

Parks in the Canadian Rocky Mountains - A spectacular UNESCO World Heritage Site composed of the provincial parks of Banff, Jasper, Kootenay, and Yoho, as well as Mount Robson, Mount Assiniboine, and Hamber - it's a paradise of glaciers, lakes, and snow-capped peaks that will take your breath away. Whistler - A famed ski-resort town just two hours from Vancouver, it is regarded as one of the top winter sports destinations in Canada.

Vancouver Island is a big picturesque island known for its outstanding marine biodiversity and the Pacific Rim National Park.

Gros Morne National Park is a distant and spectacular national park in Newfoundland that is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its natural beauty.

Churchill is the world's polar bear capital, and there's no better spot to witness these beautiful beasts in person.

Vieux-Quebec (Old Quebec) - The colourful citadel on the hill is a magnificent UNESCO World Heritage Site that is considered one of Canada's hidden beauties.

CANADA CURRENCY

The Canadian dollar, shortened as CAD or C$, is the country's currency. It is currently valued significantly less than the US dollar, often about 70-80 US cents. The CAD is divided into the following denominations:

  • $1 (coin)
  • $2 (coin)
  • $5 (coin)
  • $10 (coin)
  • $20 (coin)
  • $50 (coin)
  • $100 (coin)
Some places at the US/Canada border take US cash, however relying on this and simply bringing US dollars is not a good idea. Almost every shop/business takes credit or debit cards, and ATMs are widely available, so you won't need to bring huge quantities of cash. ATMs often impose a withdrawal fee of $2-5 CAD.
