Canada Airports

Canada is one of the most prosperous countries in the world. Vancouver, on the country's west coast, is the most liveable city in the world according to many studies. Canada is the second largest country in the world after Russia, with 38 million inhabitants, and one of the most densely populated countries in the world. The capital of Canada is Ottawa and most of the people live in the city of Toronto. Both English and French are spoken in Canada, but it is the official language in Quebec, France.
Canada is known for its beautiful and expansive nature, with Banff National Park, Jasper National Park, and Vancouver Island in the western part of the country. In eastern Canada, you can visit the spectacular Niagara Falls.

Canada's two busiest airports are Toronto, Vancouver, Montreal, and Calgary, with the largest airlines Air Canada and WestJet.

Toronto Pearson Airport

Toronto Pearson Airport is Canada's largest and busiest airport and is home to state-owned Air Canada. The airport is also used extensively by Sunwing Airlines and the low-cost carrier WestJet. Toronto Pearson Airport is named after former Prime Minister Leicester B. Pearson won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1957. The airport consists of two terminals (T1 and T3) and five runways. Toronto has a second airport used for Regent flights called Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport

Vancouver Airport

Vancouver International Airport is Canada's second busiest airport, with three terminals and three runways. It is Western Canada's largest airport, and it is popular with both visitors and business travellers. The airport is close to Vancouver Island, as is the border with the United States. The city centre is reachable via metro and cab.

Where is Vancouver Airport located?

Montreal-Trudeau Airport

Montreal Trudeau Airport is an airport in the city of Montreal, Canada. The airport is named after former politician Pierre Elliott Trudeau, who was born in Montreal in 1919. The airport is the third busiest in the country, with Toronto Pearson and Vancouver airports being the largest in Canada. Numerous flights are operated by Air Canada and Air Transat. Montreal-Pierto Airport is the largest airport in the autonomous province of Quebec, and French is the only official language in the state.
